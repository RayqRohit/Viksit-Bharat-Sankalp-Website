About

“Viksit Bharat Sankalp”
National Elocution Competition

The Viksit Bharat Sankalp National Elocution Competition is a unique platform for youth to voice their vision and commitment towards a developed India by 2047. As India marches towards the centenary of its independence, this competition empowers young minds to articulate bold ideas, share inspiring stories, and embrace the spirit of Sankalp, a collective resolve for progress

India is not just witnessing change; it is becoming change. The Viksit Bharat Sankalp National Elocution Competition invites the youth of the nation to lend their voice to the journey of 11 transformational years under visionary leadership that redefined governance, development, and India’s global identity.

From monumental constitutional reforms to record-breaking infrastructure growth, from space missions that make the world look up to us, to a renewed sense of cultural pride — this is your platform to reflect, voice, and envision the India of tomorrow.

Hosted by the NAAC A++ accredited Parul University, one of India’s premier institutions in higher education and nation-building initiatives, the Viksit Bharat Sankalp National Elocution Competition scheduled from 26th to 28th September marks a powerful stride in empowering youth voices and shaping the narrative of New India’s transformative journey.

The event is expected to witness enthusiastic participation from across the nation, with competitions conducted across five zones to ensure diverse representation and nationwide reach. With over 2,000 participants anticipated, the competition will be conducted in three levels, offering a platform for voices from every corner of India. A distinguished panel of judges, comprising eminent figures from academia, public service, and media, will evaluate the performances upholding the highest standards of integrity, excellence, and fairness throughout the event.

Structure of the Competition

Zonal Participation

To ensure a truly national outreach and diverse representation, the competition will be conducted across five zones of India - North, South, East, West, and Central. This zonal model is designed to reflect the cultural and regional richness of the country, giving voice to young minds from every corner of India.

Language Categories

Participants may choose to compete in either Hindi or English, embracing India's linguistic diversity while maintaining a level playing field for all. This bilingual format promotes linguistic inclusivity, national unity, and wider participation.

University Participation Criteria

Each participating university is invited to nominate two student representatives, encouraging fair representation and equal opportunity. This selection process ensures that institutions from across the country send forward their most articulate, passionate, and informed speakers to the national stage.

Duration

Each participant will be allotted 4 to 5 minutes to deliver their speech. Within this limited time frame, participants are expected to present a well-structured, impactful, and original piece that reflects both their personal perspective and a deeper understanding of the competition's core themes.

Format & Flow

The competition will unfold over three levels: Zonal, Semi-Final, and National, offering a progressive platform for participants to advance based on merit and performance, culminating in the grand finale held at Parul University Campus, Vadodara.

Competition Format

The Viksit Bharat Sankalp National Elocution Competition is structured in a multi-tier format, designed to progressively spotlight the most articulate and visionary voices from across the country. The competition unfolds in three rounds, combining both digital accessibility and the richness of live oration.

Round 1
Digital Preliminary Round – Online
  • Participants will submit pre-recorded video speeches via a designated online portal, ensuring accessibility for students from every zone of India.
  • Each video will be reviewed and evaluated by a National Jury Panel of three distinguished experts, assessing content depth, articulation, originality, delivery style, and overall impact.
  • From each of the five zones (North, South, East, West, and Central), the top 10 participants per language category (Hindi and English) will be selected bringing the total number of finalists to 100.
  • These finalists will move forward to the offline National Zonal Finals, ensuring representation from all regions and both official competition languages.
Round 2
National Zonal Finals – Offline
  • The 100 zonal finalists will be invited to the Parul University Campus, Vadodara, for a 3-day offline competition an immersive cultural and intellectual experience.
  • Each participant will deliver their speech live on stage, in the presence of a jury panel comprising three eminent personalities from academia, public speaking, and cultural leadership.
  • Judging will be based on refined parameters including content relevance, clarity of thought, expression, and audience engagement.
  • At the conclusion of the zonal rounds, 11 top-performing speakers regardless of region or language will be chosen as the National Finalists, based on their exceptional articulation and impactful presence.
Round 3
The Grand Finale – Offline
  • The Top 11 National Finalists will return to the stage one final time to deliver a fresh, compelling speech in the Grand Finale, held at Parul University.
  • This concluding round will be judged by a distinguished national jury, including the Hon’ble Dr. Hemang Joshi Ji, Member of Parliament, adding prestige and significance to the final verdict.
  • After rigorous evaluation, the Top Elocutionist of the Nation will be crowned an orator whose voice echoes the spirit of New India and the vision of Viksit Bharat @2047.
  • The Grand Finale will stand as a tribute to 11 revolutionary years of transformative leadership, celebrated through the most powerful medium of expression the spoken word.

To ensure equitable representation and nationwide outreach, the competition is divided into five geographical zones. Each zone comprises a group of states and union territories, carefully mapped to reflect the socio-cultural and regional diversity of India. This zonal framework allows for balanced participation and encourages voices from both metropolitan and remote regions to join in the national dialogue.

Rules And Regulations

  • Participants must be enrolled as regular or private students of any recognized university across the country.
  • The speech must be delivered strictly in either English or Hindi. Usage of a mixed language medium will lead to disqualification.
  • The video submission should have a minimum duration of 3 minutes and must not exceed 4 minutes.
  • Speeches must be original, fluent, and articulated in a clear and coherent manner. Plagiarized or poorly delivered content will not be entertained.
  • Each participant is eligible to represent only one zone throughout the competition.
  • The content of the speech must remain respectful and must not include any remarks targeting any religion, community, individual, or language.
  • The decision taken by the panel of judges for shortlisting participants to the next round shall be final and binding.
